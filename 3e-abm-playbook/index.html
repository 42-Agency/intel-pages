Pilot Account Selection (Using Zoominfo)

Unlocking Your Unused Zoominfo Intent Data

You're paying for Zoominfo intent data but not using it. Here's how to activate it for ABM:

Step 1: Configure Intent Topics

In Zoominfo, set up intent monitoring for topics that signal EHS buying activity:

  • Primary Topics: EHS software, environmental compliance software, safety management systems, chemical inventory management, SDS management
  • Regulatory Triggers: OSHA compliance, EPA regulations, REACH compliance, GHS labeling, hazmat transportation
  • Competitor Research: [Your top 3 competitors], EHS platform comparison, chemical management vendors

Step 2: Build Your Initial Account List

Export accounts showing intent in the last 90 days with these filters:

Filter Criteria
Industry Chemical Manufacturing, Pharmaceutical, Retail (with chemical handling)
Revenue $500M - $10B (enterprise sweet spot)
Employee Count 2,000 - 50,000
Intent Score Medium or High (exclude Low)
Geography US, Canada, UK, EU (your serviceable regions)

Tier Your Accounts (25-50 Total for Pilot)

Not all accounts get the same treatment. Split your list based on fit score + intent score:

Tier 1: High-Touch (10-15 accounts)

Best-Fit + High Intent

Sales + Marketing coordinated outreach. Custom content. 1:1 or 1:few ads. Personal sales sequences.

Tier 2: Programmatic (15-35 accounts)

Good-Fit + Medium Intent

Automated Marketo nurtures. Account-list LinkedIn ads. Standardized content. Sales monitors engagement.

Tier 1 Qualification Criteria

  • Revenue >$1B OR employee count >5,000
  • Intent score: High in Zoominfo
  • Multiple intent topics showing (not just one)
  • Existing relationship (past demo, event attendee, content download) OR
  • Strategic fit (logo value, industry vertical leader)
Pilot Account Mix Recommendation:

12 Tier 1 accounts + 30 Tier 2 accounts = 42 total accounts. This gives you enough volume for statistical validity while keeping Tier 1 workload manageable.

Buying Committee Mapping (Tier 1 Only)

For each Tier 1 account, use Zoominfo to identify and map the buying committee:

Role Typical Title What They Care About
Economic Buyer VP/SVP of EHS, Chief Safety Officer TCO, risk reduction, compliance liability
Technical Buyer Director of EHS, EHS Manager Features, integrations, implementation
User Buyer EHS Specialists, Safety Coordinators Ease of use, daily workflows, training
IT/Security IT Director, Security Analyst Data security, SSO, compliance
Procurement Strategic Sourcing Manager Pricing, contracts, vendor risk

Target coverage: Identify at least 3-5 contacts per Tier 1 account before launching campaigns.

LinkedIn ABM Tactics

Account List Targeting Setup

LinkedIn's Company List targeting is the foundation of your ABM campaigns. Here's how to set it up:

Creating Your ABM Audiences

  1. Export from Zoominfo: Company names + LinkedIn Company URLs (if available) + domains
  2. Upload to LinkedIn: Campaign Manager > Plan > Audiences > Create Audience > Company List
  3. Create separate lists: "3E ABM - Tier 1" and "3E ABM - Tier 2"
  4. Add job function targeting: Layer with EHS, Operations, IT titles
Match Rate Reality Check:

LinkedIn typically matches 60-80% of uploaded companies. For 42 accounts, expect 25-35 to be targetable. This is why you want 40+ accounts, not just 10.

Ad Formats That Work for ABM

Lead gen forms are fine for mid-market, but enterprise ABM needs a different approach:

Recommended Format Mix

Format Use Case % of Budget
Single Image Ads Awareness (thought leadership, industry POV) 40%
Document Ads Education (reports, guides, case studies) 25%
Video Ads Trust building (customer stories, product demos) 20%
Conversation Ads Conversion (meeting requests, demos) 15%
Why NOT Lead Gen Forms for Enterprise:

Enterprise buyers don't convert on first touch. Lead gen forms optimize for quick captures, which attracts lower-intent mid-market leads. For ABM, focus on engagement and education first.

Campaign Sequence: Awareness > Engagement > Conversion

Run these campaigns simultaneously, using frequency capping to control exposure:

AWARENESS Weeks 1-12 | Single Image + Video
Build Recognition and Trust
  • Industry POV content ("The future of EHS compliance in [industry]")
  • Regulatory update summaries (OSHA, EPA changes)
  • 30-60 second video clips (customer testimonials, exec insights)
  • CTA: None or soft ("Learn more" to blog)
ENGAGEMENT Weeks 3-12 | Document Ads + Website Retargeting
Educate with Valuable Content
  • Industry benchmark reports (ungated Document Ads)
  • Case studies from similar companies (ungated)
  • Compliance checklists and frameworks
  • CTA: "Download" or "Read the case study"
CONVERSION Weeks 6-12 | Conversation Ads + Retargeting
Drive to Sales Conversation
  • Target only engaged accounts (website visitors, content consumers)
  • Conversation Ads with personalized paths
  • Retarget case study viewers with demo offer
  • CTA: "Talk to an expert" or "See it in action"

Sales-Marketing Coordination

Simple SLA for the Pilot

Keep the SLA simple for a pilot. You can add complexity later once you've proven the model.

Marketing Commits To:

  • Weekly account engagement report (which accounts are interacting)
  • Intent signal alerts within 24 hours of spike
  • Buying committee contact enrichment for Tier 1 accounts
  • Campaign performance review every 2 weeks

Sales Commits To:

  • Outreach to Tier 1 accounts showing engagement within 48 hours
  • Log all account activity in Salesforce (even if no access externally)
  • Provide feedback on account list quality weekly
  • Attend bi-weekly alignment call
Account Ownership:

Assign each Tier 1 account to a specific AE. They own the relationship even if marketing generated the activity. This clarity prevents "whose lead is it" debates.

How Sales Should Engage Intent-Showing Accounts

When an account shows intent signals or engages with campaigns, here's the playbook:

Intent Signal Response Matrix

Signal Strength Example Sales Action Timeline
High Intent Spike Zoominfo shows "High" + multiple topics Personalized outreach to champion + 2 others Within 24 hours
Content Engagement Case study download, webinar registration Connect on LinkedIn, send relevant follow-up Within 48 hours
Website Visit Pricing page, product pages (via Marketo) Trigger Marketo nurture + sales email Within 48 hours
Ad Engagement High click-through, video completion Add to sales sequence, monitor for more signals Within 1 week

Sample Outreach Template (High Intent)

"Hi [Name], I noticed [Company] has been researching EHS compliance solutions. Given your role leading [department], you might be evaluating how to [specific pain point related to their industry]. We recently helped [similar company] reduce compliance audit prep time by 60%. Would a 20-minute call to share what they did be useful?"

Feedback Loop Without Salesforce Access

Since external partners can't access your Salesforce, build a reporting workflow using Marketo + Power BI:

Weekly Sync Process

  1. Marketing exports from Marketo: All engagement by target accounts (email opens, clicks, form fills, web visits)
  2. Sales exports from Salesforce: Pipeline changes, meeting notes, deal stage updates for pilot accounts
  3. Combine in Power BI: Single dashboard showing account engagement + pipeline status
  4. Share weekly: 15-minute call to review, adjust priorities

Minimum Dashboard Views

Account engagement score (rolling 30 days)
Accounts by stage (No Touch / Engaged / Meeting Held / Opportunity)
Pipeline created from pilot accounts
Content performance by account tier
Intent signal trend by account

Measurement Framework

Account-Level vs. Lead-Level Metrics

Traditional demand gen measures leads. ABM measures accounts. Here's the shift:

Traditional (Lead-Level) ABM (Account-Level)
Number of MQLs generated % of target accounts engaged
Cost per lead (CPL) Cost per engaged account
Lead-to-opportunity rate Account-to-opportunity rate
Pipeline from leads Pipeline from target accounts
CAC CAC for enterprise segment
Why This Matters:

One enterprise account might have 5 people engaging with your content. Lead-level metrics would show 5 MQLs. Account-level shows 1 engaged account with buying committee penetration. The latter is more meaningful for enterprise deals.

What "Success" Looks Like for a 90-Day Pilot

Green Light to Scale
  • 40%+ of target accounts engaged
  • 8+ qualified meetings booked
  • $750K+ pipeline created
  • Sales feedback: "quality accounts"
  • Tier 1 outperforms Tier 2
Pause and Reevaluate
  • <20% of accounts engaged
  • <3 meetings from pilot
  • Minimal pipeline attribution
  • Sales feedback: "no different from cold"
  • No discernible tier performance difference
The Yellow Zone:

Results between these extremes suggest tweaking, not abandoning. Common fixes: refine account selection criteria, adjust content mix, increase Tier 1 personalization, extend timeline to 120 days.

90-Day Pilot Timeline

Week-by-Week Breakdown

Weeks 1-2: Foundation
Account Selection & Setup
  • Configure Zoominfo intent topics for EHS
  • Build initial account list (42 accounts)
  • Tier accounts into Tier 1 (12) and Tier 2 (30)
  • Map buying committees for Tier 1
  • Upload company lists to LinkedIn Campaign Manager
  • Create Power BI dashboard structure
  • Align with sales on account ownership
Weeks 3-4: Launch
Campaign Go-Live
  • Launch awareness campaigns (Single Image + Video)
  • Set up Marketo nurture for engaged contacts
  • Sales begins LinkedIn connection requests to Tier 1
  • First weekly sync meeting
  • Monitor delivery and reach, adjust targeting if needed
Weeks 5-8: Engage
Layer in Engagement Content
  • Add Document Ads (case studies, industry reports)
  • Begin website retargeting for engaged accounts
  • Sales outreach to accounts showing engagement
  • First intent spike responses
  • Bi-weekly review: adjust creative, budget allocation
  • Mid-pilot health check at week 6
Weeks 9-12: Convert
Conversion Push & Analysis
  • Launch Conversation Ads to engaged accounts
  • Heavy sales outreach to high-engagement accounts
  • Retarget content consumers with demo offers
  • Final data collection and cleanup
  • Compile pilot results report
  • Go/no-go decision meeting with leadership
Week 13: Decision
Pilot Review & Scale Decision
  • Present results to leadership
  • Compare to success criteria
  • If green: Plan expansion (100+ accounts, larger budget)
  • If yellow: Identify adjustments, extend pilot 30 days
  • If red: Document learnings, reallocate budget
